Technical Report Sample
Overview
 Project Title: Online Fraud Awareness Chatbot for Myanmar Consumers
 Team/Individual Name: Digital Shield
 Affiliation/Institution: Yangon Institute of Technology
Executive Summary
This report proposes the development of an AI-powered chatbot focused on online fraud
awareness, specifically tailored for consumers in Myanmar. The project addresses the
pervasive issue of online shopping scams, including fake Facebook stores and
unsolicited cash-on-delivery schemes, which are common problems in the region.
The chatbot will leverage a locally hosted Large Language Model (LLM) via Ollama and
the Deepseek model, along with a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) system with
ChromaDB, to provide accurate and timely advice on online shopping do's and don'ts,
and common scam identification. While general awareness bots exist, this project
uniquely targets specific, niche fraud challenges prevalent in Myanmar.
Future enhancements include expanding to other scam types (e.g., investment fraud)
and incorporating multimodal and multilingual support. This solution aims to empower
users with critical knowledge, fostering safer online financial transactions.
Problem Statement
The Problem
Online fraud is a rapidly escalating problem in Myanmar, causing significant financial
losses and erosion of public trust in digital platforms. A prevalent issue involves
fraudulent online shopping practices, such as fake Facebook stores that vanish after
payment and the coercive tactic of sending unsolicited goods for cash-on-delivery
collection. Consumers, particularly those new to e-commerce, often lack the specific
knowledge to identify and avoid these sophisticated scams.
Current Gap
Existing fraud awareness efforts are often generic or not readily accessible, failing to
address the nuanced and rapidly evolving fraud tactics specific to the Myanmar online
landscape. There is a critical lack of an always-available, interactive resource that can
provide real-time, context-specific advice on identifying and mitigating these localized
scams. General awareness bots lack the specific niche focus required for this problem.
Why a AI/Chatbot?
An AI-powered chatbot offers a highly accessible, scalable, and user-friendly solution to
deliver targeted online fraud awareness. Its ability to provide instant, personalized
guidance makes it ideal for educating a broad audience about specific scams,
empowering consumers to make safer online shopping decisions and avoiding financial
harm.
Proposed Solution
Chatbot Description
Our proposed AI-powered chatbot, tentatively named "Dut Guardian" (Myanmar
Guardian), will serve as a dedicated online fraud awareness assistant for Myanmar
consumers. Users will interact through a web-based interface, asking questions about
suspicious online activities or seeking advice on safe shopping practices. The chatbot
will process queries using its local LLM and RAG system to deliver precise, actionable
information on online shopping do's and don'ts, and common scam identification.
Key Features
 Online Shopping Safety Tips: Provides guidance on secure payment methods,
verifying seller legitimacy, and understanding return policies.
 Scam Identification: Helps users identify common online shopping scams, such
as fake Facebook stores and unsolicited cash-on-delivery parcels.
 Do's and Don'ts: Offers clear, concise advice on safe online purchasing
behaviors.
 Fraud Reporting Guidance: Directs users on how and where to report
fraudulent activities to relevant authorities or platforms.
 Scenario-Based Learning: Presents hypothetical scam scenarios and teaches
users how to respond safely.
Benefits
"Dut Guardian” uniquely improves online safety by providing highly specific, localized
fraud awareness knowledge directly addressing the prevalent scams in Myanmar. This
targeted approach fills a critical gap in general awareness efforts, empowering users to
actively protect themselves against evolving online threats.
Related Work
Existing Solutions
While various general AI chatbots offer broad cybersecurity advice, and some platforms
provide general fraud alerts (e.g., banking apps), there is no existing AI-powered
chatbot specifically designed as a niche solution for online shopping fraud awareness,
especially with a focus on Myanmar's unique scam landscape. International solutions
like those from cybersecurity firms tend to focus on enterprise-level threat intelligence
rather than consumer-specific, localized fraud education.
Enhancements
Our project delivers a novel, highly specific solution by focusing exclusively on online
shopping fraud, particularly addressing issues like fake Facebook stores and unsolicited
goods in Myanmar. This niche focus, combined with the use of a locally hosted LLM and
RAG, creates a unique, accessible, and highly relevant tool that goes beyond general
awareness to tackle concrete, prevalent consumer problems.

Integration
The system will integrate Ollama for hosting the Deepseek LLM locally, ChromaDB for
building and querying the Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) knowledge base, and
Streamlit for developing the user-friendly chatbot interface.
Proposed Technology Stack
Core Technologies
 AI/ML Models: Deepseek LLM via Ollama for robust local natural language
understanding and generation. This model is chosen for its optimal performance
and resource efficiency for local deployment.
 Frameworks: Streamlit for rapid development of the interactive web-based
chatbot user interface.
 Database: ChromaDB will serve as the vector database for building and
managing the Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) knowledge base, enabling
efficient retrieval of relevant information for specific queries.
 Programming Languages: Python (primary language for backend logic, Ollama
interaction, RAG implementation, and Streamlit application).
Deployment
Local Hosting: The entire solution is designed for local hosting via Ollama, minimizing
dependency on cloud infrastructure and ensuring data privacy for sensitive scam-
related queries.
Use Cases & Scenarios
Scenario 1: Identifying a Fake Facebook Store
 A user sees an advertisement for a highly discounted smartphone on a new
Facebook page in Myanmar and is suspicious.
 User Input: "I saw a great deal on a phone on Facebook, but the page looks
new. How can I tell if it's a scam like those fake stores?"
 Chatbot Response: "That's a smart question! Many fake Facebook stores
operate by luring customers with unbelievable deals. Look for these red flags:
very new page creation date, no customer reviews or only generic ones, requests
for payment outside Facebook's secure systems (e.g., direct bank transfer to a
personal account), and a lack of contact information. Always prioritize cash-on-
delivery only after inspecting the goods, if possible. Would you like tips on safe
online payment?"
Scenario 2: Unsolicited Cash-on-Delivery Parcel
 A user receives a call for a cash-on-delivery parcel they don't remember ordering,
a common scam in Myanmar.
 User Input: "Someone delivered a package to my house, demanding cash, but I
never ordered it. What should I do about these 'sending goods without ordering'
scams?"
 Chatbot Response: "This is a common tactic known as unsolicited cash-on-
delivery fraud. Do NOT accept the package or pay for it. Politely refuse delivery,
stating you did not order it. Never feel pressured into paying for something you
didn't initiate. We recommend blocking the caller's number if they persist. Have
you checked your recent order history with known retailers?"
Evaluation Plan
Success Metrics
 Information Accuracy: Measured by the chatbot's ability to correctly provide
relevant advice on online shopping fraud scenarios.
 User Engagement: Assessed by the number of interactions, session duration,
and repeat users.
 User Satisfaction: Evaluated through simple in-chat feedback or post-interaction
surveys on helpfulness and clarity.
 Scam Identification Rate: Potentially measured by tracking user reports of
detected scams after chatbot interaction (if a reporting feature is integrated).
Evaluation Tools
 Manual Review: Human experts will periodically review chatbot responses for
accuracy and relevance.
 User Feedback Forms: Short surveys within the Streamlit interface to gather
quantitative and qualitative feedback.
 Conversation Logs: Analysis of anonymized chat logs to identify common
queries, areas of confusion, and popular features.
Future Enhancements
 Expansion to Other Scams: Extend the knowledge base and functionalities to
cover other significant fraud types, such as investment scams, job scams, and
lottery scams.
 Multimodal Support: Incorporate capabilities for users to upload images (e.g.,
suspicious screenshots) or voice notes for analysis.
 Multilingual Support: Implement full support for Myanmar language in addition
to English, ensuring broader accessibility and impact.
 Community Reporting: Potentially integrate a feature for users to anonymously
report new scam tactics, contributing to a real-time threat intelligence database.
 Partnerships: Seek collaborations with local law enforcement or consumer
protection agencies to enhance reporting mechanisms.
Challenges
Challenges
 Data Collection & Currency: Continuously updating the knowledge base with
new and evolving fraud tactics specific to Myanmar.
 Local LLM Performance: Ensuring the locally hosted Deepseek LLM maintains
optimal performance and response speed on consumer-grade hardware.
 User Trust & Adoption: Convincing users, especially those less tech-savvy, to
trust and regularly use the chatbot for sensitive information.
 Misinformation Risk: Preventing the chatbot from inadvertently providing
inaccurate or harmful advice.
Mitigation Strategies
 Dedicated Research & Community Input: Establish a process for ongoing
research into new scams and integrate user-reported data (verified) into the
knowledge base.
 Resource Optimization: Optimize the Deepseek model for efficient inference
and provide clear system requirements for users to host it effectively.
 Clear Value Proposition & Outreach: Conduct targeted outreach campaigns
emphasizing the chatbot's benefits and ensure user-friendly design.
 Rigorous Testing & Human Oversight: Implement comprehensive testing
protocols and a feedback loop for human review of problematic responses,
especially for sensitive advice.
